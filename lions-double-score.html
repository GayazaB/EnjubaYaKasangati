A Symphony of Service: The Double Score for Kasangati Greater Lions

By Serwadda Sammy | MAWULE, KATADDE | April 29, 2026 Share on WhatsApp

MAWULE — On the sun-drenched weekend of Saturday, April 25th, the air in Kasangati carried more than just the scent of rain and earth; it carried the spirit of a legacy reborn. In a historic display of growth and commitment, the Lions Club of Kasangati Greater celebrated a "Double Score," presenting two new vibrant clubs to the international family of service.

The induction of the Lions Club of Nangabo and the Kasangati Greater II Leo Club stands as a towering achievement in this 2025/2026 Lionistic year. With over 20 charter members in each fold, the pride has not just grown—it has roared.

The Dawn of Service: Solidarity at Benjamin’s House

Before the evening’s gold and blue pageantry, the heart of Lionism was put into motion. Led by President Lion Patrick Muganga, a delegation journeyed to the quiet hills of Mawule to visit The Benjamin’s House, Uganda.

Benjamin's House operates through a dedicated and life-changing framework known as the "Four Rs": Rescue, Rehabilitation, Restoration, and Resettlement. Bishop Basalirwa Cosmas Ricky noted that the home uses nutrition, sports, and spiritual mentorship to "sweat out" the influence of drugs, returning young men to society as skilled citizens.

We are here to create an inspirational start. We aren't just joining a club; we are finding our purpose. — Charter Leo President Carlos Kimbowa

The Grand Gala: A Night of White, Gold, and Blue

As evening fell, the Kasangati Resort Centre was transformed into a cathedral of fellowship. The hall buzzed as Chief Guest, District Governor Lion Alen Tush Naturinda, took the podium.

"Kasangati Greater does not settle for less. To see a mega-objective scored so early in the year is a milestone of excellence." — District Governor 411B, Ln Tush Naturinda

DG Alen commended Guiding Lions Rodgers Kamuhanda and Dan Aleen for their tireless mentorship.

True leadership is service that transforms lives. Seeing these young Leos and Lions step up gives us great hope for the future of lionism. — Guest of Honor,Dr. Colonel Ln Barbra ALangat

Looking Forward: A Legacy in Motion

Our mission is defined by discipline and action. We target a legacy of clean water and primary education for Mawule. — Charter President Lion Joseph Nyende

Nyende announced an ambitious first-year target: a 43-million-shilling project to bring clean water and scholastic supplies to a primary school in Mawule. The room responded with typical Lionistic spirit, raising over 5 million shillings in pledges on the spot.

The Mawule Mission Goal

UGX 43,000,000

Clean water & Scholastic supplies.

As the music faded and the stars rose over Kasangati, two new clubs stood ready. One mission, many hands, and a double score for humanity.
