Vox Pop: Voices from the Inauguration Arena ✍️

Enjuba ya Kasangati hit the grounds during the swearing-in layout to pull unfiltered statements, advice, and manifestos directly from stakeholders and leaders.

"I thank the people of Kasangati Town Council for trusting me with the leadership position. I promise that God before us, I’m going to lead Kasangati Town Council with integrity and kindness (obuntu bulamu). The Councilors and I are not here to consider differences in all aspects. We are here to get the manifesto from you. Guide us. Share with us what is needed. We are ready to serve. If you have any complaints, do not go to social media, come and we talk about it."

Hajji Baker Kazibwe

Newly Sworn-In Mayor, Kasangati

"Come and we serve. We are not here to please people but to serve as the law guides. If you find something you do not know, ask. My office door is always open for guidance."

Mr. Jude B. Muganga

Town Clerk, Technical Wing

"As I promised the people of Kazinga – Buyinja, we have already started engaging garbage collecting companies. We are in an Ebola pandemic. Ebola comes due to dirtiness. We are going to come to our villages and sensitize people on how to sort and handle waste. I as well call upon all the community members to stay clean."

Hon. Batte Isaac

Councilor, Buyinja – Kazinga Ward

"People of Wattuba, it is time to serve again. As we completed last term, we had a number of issues pending. I’m beginning from there. We need the Wattuba health centre issues sorted. And also look forward to having the very first USE Secondary School in Kasangati Town Council constructed from our place. This will solve an issue of many children who fail to continue with education after Primary seven."

Hon. Nakibinge Mike Bute

Councilor, Wattuba Ward

"I’m happy to talk to Enjuba ya Kasangati on this historic day of inauguration of our leaders. We request the new team that, unlike the outgoing council members, they shouldn’t be bickering and fighting developmental motions. Let them agree and give good services to the community. I as well request the enforcement team not to be harsh when collecting taxes. Let this be a peaceful term."

Omulangira Katerega

Community Elder & Resident
