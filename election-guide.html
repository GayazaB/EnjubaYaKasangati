Decision 2026: Kasangati Votes

Wednesday, February 4th, 2026 | By Enjuba News Desk

Today, the people of Kasangati Town Council head to the polls to determine the leadership that will shape the next five years (2026–2031). Voters are choosing a Mayor and councilors for 50 cells across the nine wards. These leaders will be the stewards of vital services: from healthcare and clean water to infrastructure, trade, and the collective wellbeing of our community.

The Race for Mayor: Meet the candidates vying for your vote.

Ecological Party (EP)

Bakyayita Thomas Kentos

A veteran of local politics and the current LC2 Chairperson for Nangabo Ward. Kentos is widely regarded as a consultant on local government management and environmental sustainability. He resides in Nangabo with his family.

National Unity Platform (NUP)

Ssempebwa Tony Kiyimba

Known as the first Mayor of Kasangati Town Council, Ssempebwa returns to the political stage after a term of rest. He is famously remembered for being among the first to welcome Kyagulanyi Robert Ssentamu (Bobi Wine) into politics in 2017. He resides in Gayaza.

Independent

Olive Mary Nakibuuka

The sole female candidate in the race and the outgoing First Deputy Mayor. Known for her vocal and assertive style, Nakibuuka advocates for "participatory democracy" to solve local challenges. She is a resident of Kabubbu.

National Resistance Movement (NRM)

Ssali Bruno Zambogo

A businessman focused on "protecting the gains" of the community. Ssali actively supports over 150 students through education initiatives and is the patron of Katadde Football Club. He campaigns for the "everyday person" from his home in Katadde.

Independent

Kazibwe Baker

Known as "Nyumba ya Mwamba" for his welcoming nature, Kazibwe is a newcomer to active politics but a long-time contributor to community development. He argues that Kasangati needs "job creators, not job seekers." He lives in Kitetikka Masooli.

Peoples’ Front For Freedom (PFF)

Sekalevu David

Standing for the PFF, Ssekalevu enters the race with a mission to "clean up" the town council leadership. He brings fresh ideas focused on service delivery and efficiency. He resides in Masooli.

Independent

Kakumilizi Nathan (Raptector)

The "Jobless Billionaire" and musician is one of the youngest in the race. Inspired by Bobi Wine, Nathan believes Kasangati needs youth leadership to protect it from "thugs." He is a resident of Makenke.

Independent

Zirimenya John

A former CBS FM sports journalist and former Guild President at Buganda Royal Institute. The youngest contestant in the race, Zirimenya is passionate about sports and Cristiano Ronaldo. His manifesto focuses heavily on providing study bursaries for all children.
