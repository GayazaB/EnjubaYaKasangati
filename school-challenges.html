THE BACK-TO-SCHOOL HURDLE: Why Some Classrooms Remain Empty

Published on February 24, 2026 | By Sammy Serwadda

Financial constraints have left several learners in Kasangati Town Council waiting for a chance to return to class.

KASANGATI — While the majority of schools across the country have been in session for weeks, a silent struggle is unfolding in our community. On the ground in Gayaza, Manyangwa, and Kiteezi, dozens of learners are still seen at home during school hours, their education paused by the harsh reality of financial constraints.

As a journalist moving through these wards, I have met parents who are clearly stranded. Many are balancing the rising cost of living against the mandatory requirements—school fees, uniforms, and scholastic materials—that seem to grow more expensive every year.

"I have three children," says one mother in Katadde. "Even after selling what I could, I only have half the fees for one child. The schools are strict, and I don't know where to start."

Don’t Lose Hope: Strategic Steps for Parents

While the situation is difficult, education remains the only bridge to a better future. For parents currently stuck, there are practical steps you can take today to get your children back into the classroom:

💡 Pro-Tips for Struggling Parents

  • Open Dialogue with Headteachers: Don't stay at home in silence. Visit the school and explain your situation. Many heads are willing to accept a commitment plan or partial payments.
  • Seek Out Study Bursaries: Several local schools offer "Half-Bursaries" for bright or talented students, or those from families in genuine need.
  • Review Your School Choice: Sometimes we aim for schools beyond our current means. There are excellent, affordable community schools that prioritize learning over luxury.
