THE PILLARS OF OUR SUCCESS: Gayaza C.U. SS Hails the Power of Women

KASANGATI — During the 2026 Women's Day celebrations, the leadership of Gayaza Church of Uganda Secondary School shared profound insights into the indispensable role women play in shaping both the school and the wider community.

The Head Teacher, Mr. Wamusai Richard, described a woman as "God's representative" and a "good coordinator." He emphasized that female staff members act as mothers within the school family, providing essential counseling and nurturing to students, especially those from distressed backgrounds.

80%
of student follow-ups are conducted by mothers. Mr. Wamusai noted that female parents are the backbone of the school's guardian network, ensuring consistent academic progress.
"To my fellow men out there: kindly handle these women with care. See them as 'weaker vessels'—as the Bible says—who are to be supported, cherished, loved, and recognized. Be the shoulder they can always lean on."
— Mr. Wamusai Richard, Head Teacher

A Message of Hope

The school's Director of Studies, Mr. Kibira Kenneth, and the Head Prefect joined in appreciating the modeling and guidance provided by women. Mr. Wamusai concluded with an empowering message, urging women to recognize their divine capacity to change the world.
