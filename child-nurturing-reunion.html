Nurturing the Next Generation: Lessons from the Fumbe Clan Reunion

By Sammy Serwadda, Journalist
February 2, 2026 | Kasangati, Uganda

KASANGATI ‚Äî As the long school holiday comes to a close, the question of how we nurture our children beyond the classroom has never been more vital. I recently had the privilege of attending a family reunion for the Fumbe Clan, where I witnessed a masterclass in community building and cultural preservation.

The family elders organized the day with a clear, strategic intention: to create an annual gathering where members from different families can reunite, share life updates, and introduce the newest members of the clan.

"It was more than a party; it was a classroom without walls."

The heart of the day was centered on teaching the young ones the beauty of Baganda culture. Children were guided on how to prepare traditional meals, the correct way to dress in cultural outfits, and the essential values of relating with their cousins and extended relatives.

The air was filled with the sounds of the Kiganda dance as the youth were inspired to embrace their roots. After a magnificent feast featuring a 'tribe' of local foods and drinks, the atmosphere shifted to high-energy games. From Tug of War to the focused challenge of water bottle carrying using only plates and threads, the joy was infectious.

For us at Enjuba Ya Kasangati, this event embodies the "Kingdom mindset"‚Äîseeing families come together to ensure the next generation is inspired to love their culture and stay connected. It is through these strong family bonds that we will develop a strategically thriving community.
