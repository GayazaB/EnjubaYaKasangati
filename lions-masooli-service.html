ROOTS OF HOPE: Lions and Leos Empower 600 Learners in Masooli

March 8, 2026 Share on WhatsApp Lions of Kasangati Greater and Leos with the happy learners of St. John's C.U. Masooli. (Tap to zoom)

MASOOLI — Leading up to International Women's Day, the Lions Club of Kasangati Greater together with the Leos descended upon St. John's Church of Uganda Primary School, Masooli, for a comprehensive service activity that touched the lives of over 600 children.

✨ Impact at a Glance

  • 300 Girls: Received menstrual health education and sanitary towels.
  • 300 Boys: Received hygiene talks and washing soap.
  • Environment: Fruit trees planted across the school campus.

Environmental Mentorship

Led by Lion Adrian Kamuhanda, the team planted various fruit trees around the school. To ensure sustainability, two learners from each class were selected as "Tree Guardians" to look after their class-allocated tree.

"We want the children to understand that trees provide food, shelter, and support a good climate," Adrian noted, promising that the club would return to track the trees' progress.

Menstrual Health and Hygiene

In a specialized health talk, Lion Stella Anyango (Medical Personnel) empowered the girls to embrace their body changes with confidence. The "New Voices" team demonstrated the proper use of both disposable and reusable sanitary towels.

Simultaneously, Lion Trinity and the male Lions mentored the boys on hygiene, discipline, and the importance of respecting teachers, fellow students, and the community.

"We saw that the next generation of women in Kasangati needed to be empowered. We shared the need to stay safe, abstain, and finish school before thinking about childbearing. They are the future citizens we are looking forward to."
— Lion Adrian Kamuhanda, Activity Coordinator

Chairman’s Gratitude

Mr. Muwanga Steven Kigere

L.C. 1 Chairman, Masooli: "We are happy that you have chosen to stand with us. UPE schools usually have a lot of need, and this gesture means everything to our learners."
