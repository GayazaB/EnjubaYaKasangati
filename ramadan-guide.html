THE ROAD TO RENEWAL: A Community Guide to a Fruitful Ramadan

Published on February 24, 2026 | By Sammy Serwadda

Mukiibi Shafiq, Vice Chairperson of Gayaza Market Vendors, sharing a message of faith at his stall.

GAYAZA — As the crescent moon approaches, signaling the start of the holy month of Ramadan, the spirit of preparation is visible throughout Kasangati Town Council. To understand how our community is gearing up for this sacred time, Enjuba ya Kasangati visited the bustling Gayaza Market.

Among the traders, we met Mukiibi Shafiq, a devout Muslim and the Vice Chairperson of the Gayaza Market Vendors. Surrounded by the daily hustle of his stall, Shafiq shared a message of profound spiritual reflection.

"Ramadan is more than just abstaining from food and drink; it is a time to cleanse the heart and use our time in a way that pleases Allah. I encourage my brothers and sisters to watch their actions and use this time appropriately to reap the full benefits of the fast."

Practical Tips for a Fruitful Fast

Referencing the guiding principles of the faith, Shafiq highlighted that the "true image of Ramadan" is shown through character and discipline. For those working in busy environments like markets and shops, he suggested focusing on three key areas:

1. Intentional Time Management Dedicate specific moments between work for prayer and reading the Quran to keep the spirit connected.
2. Patience in Trade Maintain a calm and honest demeanor with customers and fellow vendors, reflecting the peace of Islam.
3. Acts of Sadaqah Even a small gesture of sharing or kindness in the market can multiply the blessings of your fast.

Warm Regards to the Community

Before returning to his duties, Mukiibi Shafiq extended his heartfelt regards to his loyal customers, his fellow market vendors, and the entire Muslim community in Kasangati, Gayaza, and beyond.

"I wish everyone following Enjuba ya Kasangati a peaceful and spiritually rewarding Ramadan season. May this month bring light to our homes and our community," he concluded.
