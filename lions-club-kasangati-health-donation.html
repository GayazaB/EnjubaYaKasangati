Kasangati Greater Lions Club Gifts New Mothers, Launches 50M Hospital Theatre Refurbishment Project

By Admin | Published: June 6, 2026 | Location: Kasangati Health Centre IV
Members of the Lions Club of Kasangati Greater president gives care packs to mothers at Kasangati Health Centre IV.

June 6th, 2026 marked the official installation day for the new leadership team of the mighty Kasangati Greater Lions Club. As outgoing president Lion Patrick Muganga handed over the leadership mantle of this outstanding community service organization to incoming president Ln. Adrine Kirabo Kamuhanda, the club chose to celebrate the milestone by directly reaching out to the major public health facility within their vicinity.

The Lions arrived at the facility carrying heavily stocked essential maternal care packages containing buckets, jerrycans, laundry detergent, and baby diapers. Going a step further than domestic supplies, the organization also officially donated two modern hospital beds along with vital clinical medical equipment to boost the facility's day-to-day operational capacity.

According to incoming president Ln. Adrine Kirabo Kamuhanda, beginning her leadership year with a hospital field visit was a strategic choice. The club's signature intervention goal for the entire year will center specifically around completely overhauling and renovating the facility's highly strained operating theatre.

“We discovered that the only operation theatre here is in a very desperate state. It handles both complex obstetric emergencies like Caesarean sections for delivering mothers and general medical surgeries alike. The structure is heavily aged, necessary surgical equipment is worn out or insufficient, and the existing operating beds are in a poor state. We knew we needed to step in.”

— Ln. Adrine Kirabo Kamuhanda, Incoming President

🛠️ Hospital Operating Theatre Renovation Scope:

  • Complete restructuring, wall reinforcement, and advanced room ventilation optimization.
  • Sourcing and purchasing brand-new standard theatre beds and surgical instrumentation.
  • Future Phase: Segmenting the general in-patient ward to separate children from adult patients to cut down on pediatric health risks.
  • Total Estimated Budget: Over 50 Million Uganda Shillings (UGX).

“The job ahead of us is massive, but our immediate focus is locked on the operation theatre first,” Ln. Adrine added. “We firmly believe that through strategic local partnerships with community stakeholders, support from global Lions networks, and contributions from well-wishers, we will complete this mission successfully.”

On behalf of Kasangati Health Centre IV management, the medical officer-in-charge, Dr. Roselyn Akugizibwe, expressed profound gratitude to the Lions of Kasangati Greater for their deep-rooted commitment to social service and love for the community.

Dr. Akugizibwe highlighted the ongoing financial hurdles the facility routinely navigates to manage emergencies quickly, noting that government health subventions are often stretched too thin to address every infrastructural shortfall at once. She welcomed external community support groups to join in creating quality healthcare layouts for the over 100,000 residents across Kasangati Town Council and neighboring sub-counties who depend entirely on the facility daily.
